  • Marie Hicks,
  • Ph.D.

I am a historian of technology, gender, and modern Europe (focusing on Britain). I study how connections between national prestige, labor, and productivity define collective understandings of technological progress, and how that relates to social progress. I am particularly interested in the global history of computing. I am currently an assistant professor at Illinois Institute of Technology. I've also taught at Duke University and North Carolina State University.
